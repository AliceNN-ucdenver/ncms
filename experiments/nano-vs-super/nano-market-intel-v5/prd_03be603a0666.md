## Problem Statement and Scope  

**Problem Statement** (grounded in research premises):  
- The authentication‑services market is expanding rapidly (**R1, R3**) but is constrained by **strict compliance mandates (R2)** and escalating breach costs (**R4**).  
- Users experience **latency‑driven revenue loss (R5)** and community‑driven gaps in **adaptive, ROI‑transparent authentication** (**R9**).  
- Security analyses reveal **critical input‑validation (R14‑THR‑002) and JWT‑validation (R14‑THR‑001) weaknesses** that could be exploited.  

**In Scope** (each item cites supporting R or E):  
1. Design and implement a **NIST‑SP 800‑63B‑4 compliant authentication engine** (R2, E1).  
2. Provide **adaptive AI risk scoring** for RFID/NFC tokens in EV/EVC contexts (R9‑a, E1).  
3. Embed **ROI analytics** (latency impact, fraud‑reduction, conversion lift) (R5, R9‑b).  
4. Deploy a **microservices/event‑driven architecture** using CALM models (E1).  
5. Use **MongoDB with embedded documents** for movie data and separate review collections (E1).  
6. Enforce **short‑lived JWTs with revocation lists** (E2, THR‑001).  
7. Apply **strict input validation & parameterized queries** for all API endpoints (E2, THR‑002).  

**Out of Scope** (with justification):  
- Building a full‑featured **payment‑processing gateway** – out of scope because the PRD focuses on **identity‑service authentication**, not transaction handling (R1 does not address payments).  
- Implementing **biometric hardware modules** beyond software‑based adaptive authentication – out of scope because the current research does not cover hardware‑specific biometric standards (R9‑a is limited to AI‑driven software risk scoring).  
- Supporting **on‑premises legacy SSO protocols** (e.g., older Kerberos) beyond SAML/OIDC – out of scope because community evidence (S35‑S38) indicates market preference for modern, unified SSO, and extending legacy support would increase technical debt without clear ROI.

## Goals and Non‑Goals  

### Goals  
1. **Goal:** Deliver a **compliance‑first authentication service** that meets NIST SP 800‑63B‑4, ISO 27001, and OAIS standards. — **traced to R2, E1** because compliance baseline and architect’s CALM‑based design mandate it.  
2. **Goal:** Achieve **p95 response time ≤ 200 ms** for authentication API calls. — **traced to R5** (latency‑conversion impact) and R1 (market growth implies performance expectations).  
3. **Goal:** Realize **≥ 400 % ROI** on authentication‑workflow automation within three years. — **traced to R9‑b** (underserved ROI quantification).  
4. **Goal:** Support **≥ 10 k concurrent authenticated sessions** with **99.9 % availability**. — **traced to R1** (projected market scale) and R3 (U.S. market doubling).  

### Non‑Goals  
1. **Non‑Goal:** Implement a **full CI/CD pipeline for infrastructure provisioning** – excluded because the current scope is limited to **authentication service design**; CI/CD concerns are covered by broader platform engineering and are out of scope for this PRD (R1 does not address provisioning).  
2. **Non‑Goal:** Provide **on‑premises hardware security modules (HSM)** integration – excluded because research does not discuss hardware‑based key storage, and the primary whitespace opportunity is software‑centric adaptive authentication (R11).
